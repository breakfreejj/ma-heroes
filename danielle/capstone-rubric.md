# Hero Codex Trifold — Capstone Rubric

The Hero Codex Trifold is the culminating product of the 5-week initiative. Each student presents their trifold at the community showcase in Week 5.

## What's on the trifold

| Panel | Subject | When built |
|---|---|---|
| Origin Story (left, top) | ELA | Week 1 |
| Power Schematic (left, bottom) | Science | Week 1 |
| Stat Sheet & HQ Budget (center, bottom) | Math | Week 1 (final budget Week 4) |
| Cultural Lineage (right, top) | History | Week 1 |
| Hero Manifesto (center, top) | ELA / Civics | Week 3 |
| Community Mission (right, bottom) | Civics / SEL | Week 5 |

## Rubric

Four criteria, each scored 1–4. Maximum total: 16 points.

---

### Criterion 1 — Completeness

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| **4 — Exemplary** | All 6 panels are present, finished, and visually organized. Each panel includes the required writing component. |
| **3 — Proficient** | 5 of 6 panels are present and finished. Missing or incomplete panel does not undermine the whole. |
| **2 — Developing** | 3–4 panels are present. Some writing components are missing or unfinished. |
| **1 — Beginning** | Fewer than 3 panels are present, or the writing is largely absent. |

---

### Criterion 2 — Evidence-Based Reasoning

Does the trifold show that the student used the math, science, and history they learned?

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| **4 — Exemplary** | Stat Sheet uses numbers with appropriate units AND a stated comparison; Power Schematic cites a real biological/physical mechanism AND a limit; Cultural Lineage names at least 2 specific traditions with stated reasons. |
| **3 — Proficient** | Stat Sheet uses numbers with units; Power Schematic explains a mechanism; Cultural Lineage names traditions with some reasoning. Minor gaps in evidence. |
| **2 — Developing** | Some panels have numbers, mechanisms, or sources, but reasoning is thin or unsupported. |
| **1 — Beginning** | Trifold relies on assertion rather than evidence. Stats have no units; powers have no mechanism; lineage is unspecified. |

---

### Criterion 3 — Voice & Authenticity

Does the work sound like *this student*, not a copy of someone else?

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| **4 — Exemplary** | Origin Story and Manifesto are written in the student's distinct voice. Word choice, rhythm, and detail are specific and unmistakably theirs. The hero feels meaningfully connected to the student's experience or perspective. |
| **3 — Proficient** | Writing has a consistent voice. Some lines or details feel personal and earned. The hero feels coherent. |
| **2 — Developing** | Writing is generic or borrowed-sounding. Hero feels like a copy of an existing character without significant reimagining. |
| **1 — Beginning** | Writing is incomplete or copy-pasted from a source. No discernible authorial voice. |

---

### Criterion 4 — Community Connection

The "Return with the Elixir" stage is what makes this curriculum more than fan fiction.

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| **4 — Exemplary** | Community Mission names a *specific, realistic action* the student commits to. Connects clearly to the hero's manifesto and to a real community need. Honest, not performative. |
| **3 — Proficient** | Community Mission names an action and connects to the hero's mission. May be somewhat general but is plausible and student-owned. |
| **2 — Developing** | Community Mission is vague or aspirational without specifics ("be a good person"). |
| **1 — Beginning** | Community Mission is missing or unrelated to the rest of the trifold. |

---

## Scoring Summary

| Total Score | Performance Level |
|---|---|
| **14–16** | Exemplary — ready to be a showcase exemplar for next year |
| **11–13** | Proficient — meets the goals of the initiative |
| **7–10** | Developing — partial mastery; conference with student |
| **4–6** | Beginning — significant revision needed |

## Grading notes for justice-involved settings

- **Effort and growth matter more than polish.** A trifold that took genuine work but is rough-looking is worth more than a slick one that was phoned in. Score Criterion 3 (Voice) generously when authenticity is present.
- **Avoid grading the hero's morality.** A student whose hero operates outside the law is engaging with the curriculum's core question (Week 3). That's good. Don't penalize for "villainous" choices unless the student didn't engage with the manifesto's reasoning.
- **The Community Mission is the high bar — and the most important.** If a student writes something real and specific here (e.g., "I'll write a letter to my younger cousin every week so he knows someone sees him"), that alone deserves a 4 on Criterion 4 regardless of the rest of the trifold.
- **Conference, don't just score.** Hand each student a copy of their rubric with one specific note per criterion. If possible, do a brief 1-on-1 after the showcase.

## Showcase presentation (optional add-on score)

If your program includes a presentation component at the showcase, add a 5th criterion:

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| **4** | Student presented their trifold to at least 3 audience members. Could answer questions about their reasoning. Made eye contact at least once with an unfamiliar adult. |
| **3** | Student presented to 1–2 audience members. Could answer most questions. |
| **2** | Student stood with their trifold but did not initiate presentation. Responded when approached. |
| **1** | Student did not present or attend the showcase. |

*This criterion is specifically about the "return" — bringing the work back to the community. For students who struggle with public presentation, partner them with a mentor or have them record a video pitch instead. Do not penalize for not speaking aloud if a written alternative was produced.*
