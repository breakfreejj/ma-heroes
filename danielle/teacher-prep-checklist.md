# Teacher Prep Checklist

A timeline for preparing to launch the 5-week Superheroes Initiative. Designed for the lead teacher or program coordinator. Adjust as your school's procurement timeline requires.

---

## 4 weeks before launch

- [ ] Read the full Week 1 curriculum (`superhero-initiative.md` or the website) end-to-end.
- [ ] Read the `capstone-rubric.md` so you know what students are building toward.
- [ ] Confirm your roster (number of students, age range, IEPs / 504s, reading levels). Identify which differentiation tier each student will likely need.
- [ ] Submit `materials-list.md` to your school admin for budget approval, OR launch a Donors Choose project using the framing in that file.
- [ ] Reserve any classroom equipment you'll need (projector, large maps, etc.).
- [ ] Identify 2–3 potential **community speakers for Week 4** (Allies, Mentors & The Team). Examples: formerly incarcerated mentor, community organizer, EMT or firefighter, civil rights attorney, local artist. Start scheduling now — these calendars fill up.

---

## 2 weeks before launch

- [ ] Order books or place library holds:
  - [ ] *Miles Morales: Spider-Man* — Jason Reynolds
  - [ ] *Long Way Down* — Jason Reynolds
  - [ ] *The Hate U Give* — Angie Thomas
  - [ ] *American Born Chinese* — Gene Luen Yang (graphic novel)
  - [ ] *Ms. Marvel: No Normal Vol. 1* — G. Willow Wilson
- [ ] Order or gather science lab supplies (see `materials-list.md`).
- [ ] Print or prepare:
  - [ ] Photocopied excerpts for ELA (one packet per student)
  - [ ] Hero Stat Cards (Math Day 1)
  - [ ] Boston-area maps (Math Day 2)
  - [ ] Punnett square worksheets (Science Day 2)
  - [ ] World Heroes card set — 6 cards (History Day 1)
  - [ ] American comic covers + context cards (History Day 2)
  - [ ] Harriet Tubman primary source packet (History Day 3)
  - [ ] MA Hero card set — 6 cards (History Day 4)
- [ ] Send the **family/guardian intro letter** (template can be added to this project).
- [ ] Confirm at least one Week 4 community speaker.

---

## 1 week before launch

- [ ] Acquire cardboard trifolds (or large boxes for cutting).
- [ ] Sort and organize all materials by week — labeled bins or folders.
- [ ] Walk through the Week 1 lesson sequence in your head. Identify which lesson you're most nervous about and rehearse the mini-lesson aloud.
- [ ] Plan classroom layout — group seating for jigsaws and pair work; clear space for the science labs.
- [ ] Prepare your own "teacher sample" Codex Trifold panel for the Origin Story. Modeling matters. Make it imperfect on purpose — show students you took a risk.
- [ ] If using tech: test QR code generation workflow.

---

## Day before launch

- [ ] Print the Day 1 lesson plans for each subject (use the "Print this lesson" button on the website).
- [ ] Confirm strawberries are fresh (Science Day 1).
- [ ] Pre-chill the rubbing alcohol in an ice bucket.
- [ ] Set up classroom: Do Now prompt on the board, materials staged, student folders ready.
- [ ] Take a deep breath. This work matters.

---

## Each Monday before a new week

- [ ] Quickly skim the upcoming week's 4 days in the website.
- [ ] Pull this week's printables and lab supplies.
- [ ] Check in on the community speaker (Week 4) or showcase logistics (Week 5).

---

## Week 4 — Allies, Mentors & The Team

This is the week the community speakers come in. Specific prep:

- [ ] Final confirmation with each speaker — date, time, parking, school entry procedure.
- [ ] Brief the speakers: students are 12–19, the theme is "Allies, Mentors & The Team," and the question is "Why does no hero stand alone?" Ask them to share an ally/mentor in their own story.
- [ ] Prepare student questions in advance so silence doesn't happen.
- [ ] Thank-you cards — give students 5 minutes after each visit to write one.

---

## Week 5 — Showcase prep

The final product is the trifold + a community showcase. Specific prep:

- [ ] **3 weeks before showcase:** Invite families, school staff, community members, mentors from Week 4. Use a printed flyer and a digital message.
- [ ] **2 weeks before:** Confirm space — gymnasium, library, or community center. Tables for trifolds (one per student or shared). Refreshments if budget allows.
- [ ] **1 week before:** Practice student presentations. Each student rehearses their 90-second pitch with a partner.
- [ ] **Day of showcase:**
  - [ ] Set up trifolds 1 hour early.
  - [ ] Photograph each trifold for posterity (with student permission).
  - [ ] Have copies of the rubric available for students to receive.
  - [ ] **Most important:** make sure every student who shows up gets to present to at least one adult who isn't their teacher. The "return with the elixir" only works if the community is actually present to receive it.

---

## Post-program

- [ ] Score trifolds using `capstone-rubric.md`.
- [ ] Hold individual conferences if possible — 5 minutes per student to deliver the rubric and one specific note per criterion.
- [ ] Photograph the strongest trifolds (with permission) as exemplars for next year.
- [ ] Send thank-you cards to community speakers and showcase guests.
- [ ] Reflect: write 1–2 pages on what worked, what didn't, and what you'd change. File this with the curriculum for the next teacher.
- [ ] Return trifolds to students. They keep them. The artifact belongs to them.

---

## Things that will go wrong (and don't panic)

- **A student refuses to write an origin story.** That's fine. Offer the graphic organizer instead, or ask them to dictate it to a partner. The point isn't the writing; it's that they were seen as the author.
- **A lab goes sideways.** The strawberry DNA lab is robust, but if it fails, talk through what *would* have happened. The point is the concept, not the spectacle.
- **A community speaker cancels.** Have a backup — even a recorded video interview with a community figure can carry the lesson.
- **A student's trifold is incomplete on showcase day.** Let them present anyway. Incompleteness is real; pretending otherwise wastes the moment. Score afterward.
- **A discussion gets heated** (especially in Week 3 on heroes vs. villains). Good. That means it landed. Refer back to the framing: *who's holding the pen?*
