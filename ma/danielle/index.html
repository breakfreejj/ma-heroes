Capstone Product — The Hero Codex Trifold

A cardboard trifold (low-cost, no tech required) that each student builds across the 5 weeks. Every Friday adds a panel. By Week 5, each trifold is the centerpiece of a community showcase.

Materials per student: 1 cardboard trifold (~$3 or cut from a large box), construction paper, lined paper, markers, glue stick, scissors. Optional tech: QR code linking to a phone-recorded pitch video.

Design Notes for Justice-Involved Youth

  • Position students as heroes, not as those waiting to be saved. The curriculum frames identity as authorship — they hold the pen.
  • Villain analysis is intentional. Treating students as capable of nuanced moral reasoning (Killmonger, Magneto) respects them as adults.
  • Real heroes anchor Week 4. Community speakers — formerly incarcerated mentors, organizers, EMTs — model the "return" stage.
  • Trauma-informed by design. The abyss stage (Week 3) is included, not skipped, but students choose how much of their own story they share.
